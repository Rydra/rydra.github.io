    Videogame roadmap for children

    Jan 12, 20256 min read

    • videogames

    Videojuegos por edades

    La edad adecuada para introducir a tu hijo a los videojuegos depende de su desarrollo y del tipo de juegos que elijas. Aquí tienes algunas pautas:

    De 2 a 3 años

    • Opciones: Juegos simples y educativos diseñados para tabletas o dispositivos táctiles.
    • Ejemplo: Juegos que enseñan colores, formas o vocabulario.
    • Objetivo: Estimular habilidades motoras y cognitivas básicas.

    De 4 a 6 años

    • Opciones: Juegos más interactivos, como los que enseñan resolución de problemas o coordinación.
    • Ejemplo: Juegos como Lego Duplo World o Animal Crossing.
    • Objetivo: Fomentar la creatividad y el aprendizaje sin presión.

    De 7 años en adelante

    • Opciones: Juegos familiares que introducen mecánicas más complejas, pero manteniendo un contenido apropiado.
    • Ejemplo: Mario Kart, Minecraft en modo creativo o Pokémon.
    • Objetivo: Desarrollar habilidades estratégicas, trabajo en equipo y creatividad.

    Consideraciones

    1. Control parental: Supervisa el contenido y la cantidad de tiempo que juega.
    2. Tiempo limitado: Asegúrate de equilibrar los videojuegos con otras actividades, como juegos al aire libre.
    3. Juega con ella: Es una forma fantástica de compartir tiempo y enseñarle valores como paciencia y trabajo en equipo.

    Roadmap

    Here’s a roadmap of video games your daughter can explore as the child grows, designed to match her developmental stages and interests. This plan emphasizes age-appropriate games that are fun, educational, and gradually introduce more complex gameplay mechanics.

    Ages 3–5: Early Exploration

    • Focus: Simple controls, bright visuals, forgiving gameplay, and creativity.
    • Platforms: Retro consoles, modern devices with kid-friendly options.

    Suggested Games:

    1. Mario Paint (SNES)
      • Encourages creativity with drawing and music creation.
    2. Kirby’s Dream Land (Game Boy)
      • A simple platformer with forgiving mechanics.
    3. Paw Patrol: Mighty Pups Save Adventure Bay (Modern Consoles)
      • Easy, story-driven gameplay with beloved characters.
    4. Sesame Street Games (Various Platforms)
      • Educational games focused on letters, numbers, and basic problem-solving.
    5. LEGO Duplo World (Mobile/Tablets)
      • Encourages creativity, problem-solving, and fun building.

    Ages 6–8: Building Confidence

    • Focus: Slightly more complex controls, puzzle-solving, and light storytelling.
    • Platforms: Modern consoles (Switch, PlayStation, Xbox), PC.

    Suggested Games:

    1. Super Mario Odyssey (Switch)
      • An exploration-based platformer with a friendly difficulty curve.
    2. Animal Crossing: New Horizons (Switch)
      • Introduces life simulation and creativity in a stress-free environment.
    3. Minecraft (Creative Mode) (Various Platforms)
      • Encourages creativity, exploration, and basic problem-solving.
    4. LEGO Games (Various Platforms)
      • Fun cooperative play with light puzzles and platforming (e.g., LEGO Harry Potter, LEGO Star Wars).
    5. Pokémon Sword/Shield (Switch)
      • Simple turn-based RPG with exploration and collecting.

    Ages 9–12: Developing Skills

    • Focus: Strategy, teamwork, and slightly more challenging gameplay.
    • Platforms: Expanding to PC and advanced console games.

    Suggested Games:

    1. Mario Kart 8 Deluxe (Switch)
      • Fun multiplayer with simple controls but enough challenge to improve skills.
    2. The Legend of Zelda: Breath of the Wild (Switch)
      • Open-world exploration with problem-solving and light combat.
    3. Splatoon 3 (Switch)
      • A kid-friendly introduction to online multiplayer shooters.
    4. Minecraft (Survival Mode) (Various Platforms)
      • Adds strategy and resource management to the creative sandbox.
    5. Stardew Valley (Various Platforms)
      • Introduces time management and life simulation mechanics.

    Ages 13–15: Exploring Complex Gameplay

    • Focus: Strategic thinking, narrative-driven games, and cooperative/competitive play.
    • Platforms: PC and consoles with access to broader libraries.

    Suggested Games:

    1. Portal 2 (PC/Consoles)
      • A puzzle-platformer that challenges logic and creativity.
    2. The Sims 4 (PC/Consoles)
      • A life simulation game encouraging creativity and planning.
    3. Hollow Knight (PC/Consoles)
      • A beautifully challenging platformer with deep exploration.
    4. Civilization VI (PC/Consoles)
      • Strategy and decision-making on a global scale.
    5. Co-op Games (Various Platforms)
      • Fun multiplayer games like Overcooked, Keep Talking and Nobody Explodes, or Human: Fall Flat.

    Ages 16–18: Mastering Complex Games

    • Focus: Narrative-driven experiences, advanced mechanics, and teamwork in competitive settings.

    Suggested Games:

    1. The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt (PC/Consoles)
      • A rich RPG with deep storytelling and complex choices.
    2. Dark Souls or Elden Ring (PC/Consoles)
      • Challenging games that reward patience and strategy.
    3. Among Us (PC/Consoles)
      • A social deduction game encouraging teamwork and critical thinking.
    4. Fortnite or Valorant (PC/Consoles)
      • Competitive games to practice teamwork and fast reflexes.
    5. Disco Elysium (PC/Consoles)
      • A mature RPG with philosophical storytelling for older teens.

    Tips for the Roadmap

    • Flexibility: Let her interests guide the journey. If she loves building, explore sandbox games; if storytelling fascinates her, look for narrative-driven games.
    • Co-op Play: Play games together to encourage bonding and introduce her to new challenges in a supportive way.
    • Content Awareness: Keep an eye on age ratings (ESRB/PEGI) and themes as she progresses to more mature games.
    • Encourage Balance: Games are a tool for learning and entertainment but should be balanced with other hobbies and activities.

    This roadmap allows her to grow her skills, interests, and love for gaming in a structured and enjoyable way.

    SNES and GB specific

    Here’s a curated list of kid-friendly games for the SNES and Game Boy that are fun, age-appropriate, and timelessly enjoyable:

    SNES

    1. Super Mario World

      • A classic platformer with vibrant worlds, simple controls, and delightful gameplay.

    2. The Legend of Zelda: A Link to the Past

      • A kid-friendly adventure filled with exploration, puzzles, and an engaging story.

    3. Donkey Kong Country

      • Beautiful graphics, catchy music, and a challenging but fair platforming experience.

    4. Kirby Super Star

      • A variety-packed game with multiple modes, perfect for younger players.

    5. Yoshi’s Island: Super Mario World 2

      • A visually charming platformer with creative mechanics.

    6. Disney’s Aladdin

      • A fun adaptation of the movie, with colorful graphics and accessible gameplay.

    7. Super Bomberman 2

      • Simple, fun, and great for multiplayer.

    8. Pocky & Rocky

      • A cute and cooperative top-down adventure game.

    9. Tiny Toon Adventures: Buster Busts Loose!

      • A lighthearted platformer based on the popular animated series.

    10. Super Turrican

      • Action-packed, but still accessible for older kids.

    Game Boy

    1. Pokémon Red/Blue/Yellow

      • Easy to learn and endlessly engaging, perfect for introducing RPG mechanics to kids.

    2. Kirby’s Dream Land

      • A simple and forgiving platformer great for young children.

    3. Tetris

      • A timeless puzzle game that helps develop logical thinking.

    4. The Legend of Zelda: Link’s Awakening

      • A charming and kid-friendly introduction to adventure games.

    5. Super Mario Land 2: 6 Golden Coins

      • A fun and slightly easier Mario platformer.

    6. Donkey Kong (1994)

      • A puzzle-platformer with clever mechanics.

    7. Wario Land: Super Mario Land 3

      • A quirky spin on platforming with lots of charm.

    8. Metroid II: Return of Samus

      • Slightly more challenging, but rewarding for older kids.

    9. Disney’s DuckTales

      • A light and fun platforming adventure.

    10. Harvest Moon GB

      • A relaxing farming simulator that’s great for creativity and patience.

    These games provide hours of fun while being accessible to kids in terms of difficulty and content.
